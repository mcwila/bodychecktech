BodyCheck Tech

Due to incorrect form amid deadlift, a majority of the population of lifters, especially novice lifters, experiences lower back pain. This can cause severe pain and even lead to surgery in some cases. This is due to the lack of feedback, which is necessary to fix incorrect movements. This impacts the health of novice deadlifters, as they do not know how to properly lexecute the desired movements. This also affects more than just novice deadlifters as tohers may have stake in this. This group includes trainers, physical therapists, and coaches, as well as those who make the products such as engineers and manufacturers. A successful solution to this problem would be to detect incorrect from while deadlifting with 100% accuracy. One opportunity to achieve this is to create a device to detect erroneous form and deliver immediate feedback to the user to allow quick adjustments.

The design consists of a strip of flex sensors that are sewn into a compression shirt that aligns with the user's spine and will track flexion of the back. When flexion past a certain degree is detected a vibration motor, which is also sewn into the shirt, will go off and notify the user that their form needs to be adjusted. The user will be able to adjust the degree of comfort in their form by overriding the degree of back flexion that is comfortable for them using an app because everyone will have a different range that works best for them due to biological factors such as femur length. This design is meant to be used when performing a conventional deadlift, where the legs are approximately shoulder-width apart and the arms are just outside the legs holding the bar, instead of using a sumo deadlift.
